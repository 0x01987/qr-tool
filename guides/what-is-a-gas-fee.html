What Is a Gas Fee?

A gas fee is the network cost required to process and validate a blockchain transaction. It helps compensate validators or miners for including your transaction on-chain.

Browse Tools Read Guide FAQ

Why gas fees exist

Gas fees prevent spam and compensate the network for computation and block space.

More complex transactions typically consume more resources and may cost more.

Why fees change

  • Network congestion
  • Blockchain used, such as Ethereum, Base, or Polygon
  • Transaction complexity
  • How quickly you want the transaction confirmed

Simple way to think about it

Gas is like paying for blockchain processing power and priority.

When demand is high, the cost to get into a block can increase.

FAQ

What is a gas fee?

It is the network fee paid to process a blockchain transaction.

Why are gas fees higher sometimes?

They often rise when network demand and congestion increase.

Do all blockchains have gas fees?

Most blockchains have some form of transaction fee, though terminology and mechanics vary.
