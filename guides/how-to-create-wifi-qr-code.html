How to Create a WiFi QR Code (iPhone & Android)

Want guests to join your WiFi without typing a password? A WiFi QR code lets anyone scan and connect instantly. This guide shows the fastest way (using InstantQR) plus practical tips for printing and sharing.

Open WiFi QR Code Generator Step-by-step FAQ

What is a WiFi QR code?

A WiFi QR code is a scannable QR code that contains your WiFi network details: SSID (network name), password, and security type (like WPA/WPA2/WPA3). When someone scans it, their phone prompts them to join the network automatically.

Why it’s useful

Perfect for homes, Airbnbs, offices, cafes, salons, events, and guest networks.

What you need

Your WiFi name (SSID), password, and security type (WPA/WPA2/WPA3 or WEP).

Fastest method

Use InstantQR’s WiFi QR Generator to generate a scannable code in seconds. Then print it or display it on a screen for guests to scan.

Step-by-step: Create a WiFi QR code

1

Find your WiFi network name (SSID)

Your SSID is the WiFi name you see when connecting. Example: HomeWifi_5G.

2

Copy your WiFi password

Make sure it’s exact (WiFi passwords are case-sensitive).

3

Choose the security type

Most networks use WPA/WPA2/WPA3. Older networks may use WEP. If your network is open (no password), choose None.

4

Generate the QR code

Open wifi-qr-code-generator.html, enter SSID + password, then generate and download the QR code.

5

Test it before sharing

Scan it with another phone (or ask a friend) to confirm it connects instantly.

Common mistake

If the QR code fails, it’s usually because the SSID or password was typed incorrectly, or the security type was selected wrong. Double-check spelling and capitalization.

How to use a WiFi QR code on iPhone

iPhone can scan WiFi QR codes and join networks quickly. Here’s the typical flow:

  1. Open the Camera app.
  2. Point at the QR code.
  3. Tap the WiFi prompt to join.

If your camera doesn’t detect the code, try scanning from a QR scanner inside another app, or move into better lighting.

How to use a WiFi QR code on Android

Most Android phones can scan WiFi QR codes using the camera or built-in QR scanner:

  1. Open the Camera (or QR scanner).
  2. Scan the QR code.
  3. Tap the connect/join prompt.

Some Android devices also let you create WiFi share QR codes inside WiFi settings, depending on the manufacturer and Android version.

Best way to print & share your WiFi QR code

  • Print size: Aim for at least 1.5–2 inches (4–5 cm) wide for reliable scanning.
  • High contrast: Dark QR code on a light background scans best.
  • Placement: Near entryways, reception desks, coffee counters, or the router.
  • Lamination: Great for cafes/Airbnb/hotels to prevent wear.
Pro tip

If you manage multiple locations (Airbnb, office, stores), generate a separate WiFi QR for each network and label it clearly (e.g., “Guest WiFi” vs “Staff WiFi”).

Security tips (recommended)

  • Use a guest network if your router supports it.
  • Rotate passwords occasionally if many people access your network.
  • Avoid posting staff WiFi publicly—use guest WiFi for customers and visitors.
  • Use WPA2/WPA3 whenever possible.

FAQ

What is a WiFi QR code?

A WiFi QR code encodes your SSID, password, and security type so users can scan and join without typing.

Can iPhone create a WiFi QR code?

iPhone can scan and join WiFi QR codes easily. Creating WiFi QR codes is typically done using a generator tool like InstantQR.

Do WiFi QR codes work on Android?

Yes. Most Android phones can scan QR codes with the camera or built-in scanner and connect instantly.

Is it safe to share a WiFi QR code?

It’s as safe as sharing your WiFi password. For public sharing, use a guest network and rotate the password occasionally.

Why is my WiFi QR code not working?

Usually it’s a typo in the SSID/password or the wrong security type selected. Double-check spelling and capitalization.
