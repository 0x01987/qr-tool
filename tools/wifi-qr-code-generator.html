Step-by-step

  1. Enter your Wi-Fi name (SSID).
  2. Select security type, usually WPA.
  3. Enter your Wi-Fi password unless the network is open.
  4. Click Generate and download your PNG.

Where to use a WiFi QR code

  • Home guest Wi-Fi
  • Airbnb and rentals
  • Cafes and restaurants
  • Offices and reception desks
  • Events and check-in tables

Tips for best results

  • Use at least 320px, or 512px+ for signs and posters.
  • Choose error correction M or H for printed materials.
  • SSID is case-sensitive, so double-check spelling.
  • Most modern networks should use WPA.

Privacy-first

QR generation runs locally in your browser and InstantQR does not intentionally store your Wi-Fi details.

FAQ

What is a WiFi QR code?

A WiFi QR code stores your SSID, password, and security type so guests can scan to connect instantly.

Does InstantQR store my WiFi password?

No. This tool runs locally in your browser and does not intentionally store WiFi credentials.

Which security type should I choose?

Most networks use WPA/WPA2/WPA3. Choose WEP only for older routers. Choose “No password” only for open networks.

What size should I use for printing?

Use at least 320px for easy scanning. For posters, 512px or larger can improve reliability.
