How this DCA calculator works

Dollar-cost averaging is a strategy where you invest a fixed amount at regular intervals instead of putting all of your capital in at once. This calculator estimates how much of an asset you would accumulate across multiple buy points and what that position would be worth at today’s price.

It is especially useful for crypto investors comparing recurring buys against a lump sum approach. Since prices move constantly, DCA can smooth your average entry over time and reduce the impact of buying at a single peak.

What the results mean

  • Total invested: The sum of all recurring purchases.
  • Total coins accumulated: The total amount of crypto bought across all periods.
  • Average cost basis: Your blended entry price across all purchases.
  • Break-even price: The price where your position would roughly be flat.
  • ROI: Your current profit or loss relative to total invested capital.

Frequently asked questions

What is a DCA calculator?

A DCA calculator estimates the outcome of investing a fixed amount at different price levels over time. It helps you understand total invested amount, accumulated coins, average cost basis, and current profit or loss.

How is average cost calculated?

Average cost is calculated by dividing total dollars invested by total coins accumulated. This gives your blended entry price across all purchases.

What is the difference between DCA and lump sum investing?

DCA spreads purchases across multiple price points, while lump sum invests the entire amount at one price. DCA can reduce timing risk, while lump sum may outperform if the asset rises immediately.

Does this crypto DCA calculator store my data?

No. This tool runs locally in your browser and does not require signup or store your inputs.
