Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about percentage calculations.

How do you calculate X percent of Y?
Multiply Y by X, then divide by 100. Example: 25% of 200 = (25 × 200) ÷ 100 = 50.
How do I calculate percent increase?
Subtract the old value from the new value, divide by the old value, and multiply by 100. Example: from 80 to 100 = ((100 − 80) ÷ 80) × 100 = 25%.
How do I calculate percent decrease?
Subtract the new value from the old value, divide by the old value, and multiply by 100. Example: from 100 to 80 = ((100 − 80) ÷ 100) × 100 = 20%.
What is reverse percentage used for?
Reverse percentage is useful when you know the final value after a discount or markup and need to find the original number.
Does this tool store my inputs?
No. The calculations run in your browser for speed and privacy.
